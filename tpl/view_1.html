What is a difference between Whey Concentrate, Whey Isolate and Whey Hydrolysate?

Whey Concentrate: A great all-around option, it contains a balance of protein, fats and carbs, making it ideal for most of people.
Whey Isolate: This is more refined, with a higher protein content and lower fats and carbs, perfect for those aiming for lean muscle mass or those with lactose sensitivity.
Whey Hydrolysate: Pre-digested for faster absorption, this is ideal for athletes or post-workout recovery.

How much protein do I need daily?

Your protein intake depends on the factors like age, activity and fitness goals. As a general guideline:
Sedentary adults: About 0.8g per kg of body weight.
Active individuals: Around 1.2g - 2g per kg of body weight.
Athletes and Bodybuilders: Up to 2.2g per kg of body weight.
